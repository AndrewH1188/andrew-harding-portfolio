About Me

  • Croydon College and London Metropolitan University Studying

    2005 - June 2010

    I spent two years studying Graphic Design with Print Making as a side study at Croydon College. I graduated from Croydon College with a Triple Distinction (3x A Levels) in Graphic Design and went on to learn new skills as well as further my knowledge and skills whilst studying Graphic Design at London Metropolitan University. During my three years studying at London Metropolitan University I decided to learn Photography and Illustration as a side study. I was very much against the machine with the illustration so would illustrate by hand.

  • Graphic Design Career Starts

    June 2010

    After five years of studying, I started my Graphic Design career with Richards Asssociates (a small local company) as an intern, this was only a few weeks after graduating from University. Richards Asssociates is where I had completed work experience with during the break from leaving school, before going to College. Whilst being an intern working at Richards Associates I studied at SCOLA (Sutton College of Learning for Adults) in the evenings to help my Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop, Flash and Dreamweaver skills progress further. Whilst attending SCOLA I also studied basic HTML and CSS as this is what was on the job spec and I had no knowledge of at the time. I completed my internship with Richards Associates and was taken on as a full time Graphic Designer. Here I would not only work for some of the big names in the conferencing business, but gain more skills and on the job knowledge as well as work as part of the most amazing team I could have wished to work with.

  • A move up north sees a change in career

    June 2012 - November 2013

    After moving up north to be with family I started working at Home Bargains as a sales assistant whilst I was looking for another role in a Graphic Design company. Whilst I was at Home Bargains I helped team members and even undertook junior management responsibilities.

  • Graphic Design career restarts

    December 2013 - June 2021

    After applying to various companies with Graphic Design vacancies I was able to get a role with a local Graphic Design firm. Here I would further my existing skills and learn new skills in Graphic Design as well as be able to do some photography and illustration. I changed companies throughout this time picking up new design and business skills and was able to use my existing skills to complete various tasks for various industries and clients that we worked with. I would also go on to work as a printer which helped understand the process as well as how outcomes are printed, and what can't be printed due to complexity and machine ability.

  • Volunteering continues

    June 2020 - Present

    I have previously volunteered with Marie Curie and St Raphael's Hospice as part of my Duke of Edinburgh's Award Gold Service and continued after I had completed this section. I wanted to get back into volunteering, and during the pandemic I saw an advert in the Quiet magazine that the British Tinnitus Association publish asking for help as a volunteer befriender. I read the details of the ad and applied, I like volunteering and wanted to give back to a charity that has helped me before. I was excited to be accepted and continue 2 years (2022) later to continue helping and supporting others through the British Tinnitus Association.

  • Studying with Code Institute

    22nd Feburary 2021 - 14th April 2022

    Due to the pandemic and job uncertainty I decided to enrol with Learning People Global who are partnered with Code Institute to learn to code, not only because this in demand, but also having worked with websites and web design it would seem natural to use my existing skills and branch out to new ventures. Having successfully completed the course at the middle of April, I have since been looking for a role as a Developer. If you have an open role for a Developer and would like to take a look at my Coded portfolio or even Contact me to discuss a role please do so. I very much look forward to hearing from and working with you.
