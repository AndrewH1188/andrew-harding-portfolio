My photography is much like my illustration, when I started out I was using film and the natural image, then as times changed this become digital, and the rise of Adobe Photoshop allowed image manipulation and touching up have become a factor as part of this process. Whilst studying photography at Croydon College I was able to photograph many urban and rural locations and add these to my image bank, then as part of the project I would manipulate and edit these to create an ideal location. Featured below are a small selection of the many photos that I have taken.

Aldgate East Double Exposure Film Photos

As part of studying at London Metropolitan University, we were able to use a pinhole camera. We were taught about double exposure that could be done using this camera in order to achieve creative images. I selected a few different locations around Aldgate East and experimented with the double exposure of the camera. I had to wait for the film to be developed before I was able to see the results. When I received the photos back and looked through them I was happy with the results that I had managed to capture. I was glad to be able to use a camera like this that is now considered as old technology in comparison to newer cameras and even smart phones.
Images taken with a Pinhole camera.

Photo Manipulation

When I studied photography whilst at Croydon College we were given the task to manipulate images. I decided to use images that I'd taken with others that I'd found online, edit and mix them together to create the above photos. I decided to use recognisable landmarks such as the London Eye, the Gherkin and Canary Wharf as a way to showcase my partial London origins, as I was born in South London. I also decided to use these landmarks to see how they would look being taken out of their current and familiar locations. These images certainly give the viewer something to think about.
Most images taken with a Nikon Coolpix camera, some images from Google.
Edited / manipulated in Adobe Photoshop.

Lego Scenes

Using some of the Star Wars micro builds I decided to put these on an astronomy calendar as a backdrop to celebrate Star Wars day on May 4th and 5th. I used the models to show the good using with the lighter background, and the bad with the darker background as well as slightly darkening the photo on my iPhone. I held the models that have the cotton to make them appear as if they are flying whilst capturing the image. With the below images I decided to integrate the figures with everyday life. I have many of these scenes that have been completed and intended to use these on my Little World of Lego site, but didn't have time to edit these ready to be used on the products featured on my site.
Images taken with a iPhone 13 camera.

GJ Electronics, iSix and iSix+ Product Photography

In order to showcase the products that GJ Electronics were selling they required photos of them. GJ Electronics would send samples of their products and I would photograph them and then edit them. The editing would consist of removing the background and replacing this with a plain white background, stitching images together as well as change colours so that all images with different colours would have the same angle and layout. These product images were used in Newsletters, Brochures, Catalogues, Packaging and across the various websites that are owned and managed by GJ Electronics.
Images taken with a Cannon DSLR camera.
Edited in Adobe Photoshop.
